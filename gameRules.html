RULES FOR
TIC-TAC-TOE

Tic-tac-toe is a two player paper-and-pencil game, also known as noughts and crosses or Xs and Os.

The game is played on a 3x3 grid. The players take turns placing their symbol (either an X or an O) on an empty square on the grid. The first player to get three of their symbols in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) wins the game. If all the squares on the grid are filled and no player has won, the game is a draw.

  1. Choose who will go first (either X or O).
  2. The first player places their symbol on an empty square on the grid.
  3. The second player places their symbol on an empty square on the grid.
  4. The first player takes their turn again, and so on, until one of the players gets three of their symbols in a row or all of the squares on the grid are filled
  5. If a player gets three of their symbols in a row, they win the game. If all the squares on the grid are filled and no player has won, the game is a draw.

That's all there is to it! Tic-tac-toe is a simple but fun game that can be enjoyed by people of all ages.
